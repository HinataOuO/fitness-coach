# Goal Compatibility, Volume Limits & Realistic Timelines

## HOW TO USE THIS FILE

This file is the coach's **mandatory reference** for every plan. Before building any plan:

1. Check goal compatibility table → flag incompatible combinations immediately
2. Apply max objective limit by level
3. Verify time horizon is realistic
4. Calculate max weekly volume → never exceed it
5. If anything fails these checks → deliver the direct incompatibility message and propose specific alternatives

---

## 1. GOAL COMPATIBILITY TABLE

### Legend
- ✅ **Compatible** — can be pursued simultaneously, plan integrates both
- ⚠️ **Partial** — possible with trade-offs, requires explicit explanation and priority choice
- ❌ **Incompatible** — cannot be meaningfully pursued at the same time, propose alternatives

| Goal A | Goal B | Compatibility | Notes |
|--------|--------|--------------|-------|
| Hypertrophy | Fat loss | ⚠️ Partial | Recomposition only — effective for beginners/detrained (L1–L2) and people with high BF (>20%). Slow progress for both goals. Intermediate/advanced: choose one. |
| Hypertrophy | Recomposition | ✅ Compatible | Recomposition IS this combination — valid, especially L1–L2 |
| Hypertrophy | Maximal strength | ✅ Compatible | Classic powerbuilding — integrable with block periodization |
| Hypertrophy | Calisthenics skills | ⚠️ Partial | Skills require low fatigue and CNS freshness. High volume hypertrophy competes. Possible with careful session ordering. |
| Hypertrophy | Endurance/Running | ⚠️ Partial | Interference effect exists but manageable. Keep running low-moderate volume. Hypertrophy gains slower. |
| Fat loss | Maximal strength | ⚠️ Partial | Strength can be maintained in deficit but not maximized. Acceptable as secondary. |
| Fat loss | Calisthenics skills | ❌ Incompatible | Aggressive cuts impair skill learning (CNS fatigue, reduced power). Maintenance calories minimum. |
| Fat loss | Endurance/Running | ✅ Compatible | Running supports caloric deficit. Don't overdo it — muscle loss risk increases. |
| Fat loss | Recomposition | ❌ Incompatible | These are the same continuum — recomposition IS fat loss + muscle retention. Choose one framing. |
| Maximal strength | Calisthenics skills | ✅ Compatible | Skills ARE strength. Fully compatible, especially weighted calisthenics. |
| Maximal strength | Endurance/Running | ⚠️ Partial | Interference effect on lower body strength. Keep running <3×/week, separate days from heavy leg work. |
| Calisthenics skills | Endurance/Running | ⚠️ Partial | High running volume fatigues lower body and reduces skill practice quality. Max 2×/week running if skills are primary. |
| Recomposition | Endurance/Running | ⚠️ Partial | Running aids fat loss side. But too much cardio in deficit slows hypertrophy. Max 2×/week moderate intensity. |
| Recomposition | Maximal strength | ⚠️ Partial | Strength gains slower in deficit but possible for beginners. Acceptable. |
| General athleticism | Any single goal | ✅ Compatible | General athleticism is by definition multi-modal. |
| Injury recovery | Any performance goal | ⚠️ Partial | Recovery is always primary. Performance goal is secondary and must not compromise rehab. |

## 2. MAXIMUM ACTIVE GOALS BY LEVEL

**This is a hard limit. Apply always.**

| Level | Max active goals | Notes |
|-------|-----------------|-------|
| **1 — Beginner** | **1 primary only** | No secondary performance goal. The only acceptable addition is general health habits (sleep, walking). Beginners cannot effectively train multiple adaptations simultaneously — motor learning alone saturates adaptive capacity. |
| **2 — Novice** | **1 primary + 1 secondary (compatible only)** | Secondary must be compatible per table above. If partially compatible: explain trade-offs explicitly before proceeding. |
| **3 — Intermediate** | **1 primary + 1 secondary** | Can handle partially compatible combinations if volume is managed. Must explain and get explicit agreement. |
| **4 — Advanced** | **1 primary + 1–2 secondary** | Can integrate partially compatible combos with proper periodization. |
| **5 — Elite** | **Up to 3 if periodized** | Requires block periodization — never concurrent at same intensity. |

**INCOMPATIBLE combinations are refused at ALL levels.** Level does not override incompatibility.

---

## 3. DIRECT INCOMPATIBILITY MESSAGE

When a user requests incompatible goals or exceeds the level limit, use this message structure — always direct, never vague:

> "Devo essere diretto: [OBIETTIVO A] e [OBIETTIVO B] non sono perseguibili contemporaneamente in modo efficace [al tuo livello / in questo lasso di tempo]. Il motivo è [1 frase chiara sul perché fisiologico]. Se cerchi di fare entrambe le cose insieme otterrai risultati mediocri su entrambi i fronti invece di risultati concreti su uno.
>
> Ecco le alternative realistiche per te:
> **Opzione 1:** [obiettivo principale] come focus, [obiettivo secondario] come mantenimento/supporto
> **Opzione 2:** [obiettivo B] prima per [timeframe], poi [obiettivo A]
> **Opzione 3:** [alternativa compatibile che soddisfa entrambe le esigenze sottostanti]
>
> Quale preferisci?"

**Never proceed without user choosing an option.**

## 5. MAXIMUM WEEKLY TRAINING VOLUME BY LEVEL

**Calculate this for every plan. Never exceed these limits.**
Based on ACSM Position Stand 2026 + ACSM Progression Models (2009).

### Sessions per week
| Level | Min sessions | Max sessions | Notes |
|-------|-------------|-------------|-------|
| L1 Beginner | 2 | 3 | Full body, short sessions. More = overreaching risk. |
| L2 Novice | 2 | 4 | Full body or upper/lower split |
| L3 Intermediate | 3 | 5 | Upper/lower or push/pull/legs |
| L4 Advanced | 4 | 6 | Split routines, higher specificity |
| L5 Elite | 5 | 7 | Fully periodized, includes active recovery |

### Sets per muscle group per week
| Level | Minimum effective | Maximum recoverable | Notes |
|-------|------------------|-------------------|-------|
| L1 | 6–8 sets | 10–12 sets | Above 12 = overreaching for beginners |
| L2 | 8–10 sets | 12–15 sets | |
| L3 | 10–14 sets | 16–20 sets | |
| L4 | 12–16 sets | 18–22 sets | |
| L5 | 14–20 sets | 22–30 sets | Only with full periodization + deloads |

### Maximum total weekly sets (all muscle groups combined)
| Level | Max total sets/week | Notes |
|-------|-------------------|-------|
| L1 | 60–80 sets | Strict limit — beyond this, adaptation decreases |
| L2 | 80–120 sets | |
| L3 | 100–160 sets | |
| L4 | 140–200 sets | |
| L5 | 180–250+ sets | Requires deloads every 3–4 weeks |

### Session duration
| Level | Optimal | Maximum | Notes |
|-------|---------|---------|-------|
| L1 | 45–60 min | 75 min | Beyond 75 min: cortisol rise, quality drop |
| L2 | 60–75 min | 90 min | |
| L3 | 60–90 min | 105 min | |
| L4 | 75–105 min | 120 min | |
| L5 | 90–120 min | 150 min | |

### Multi-goal volume penalty
When user has 2 compatible goals (e.g. hypertrophy + strength), apply a **15–20% volume reduction** per goal compared to single-goal programming, to keep total volume within recoverable range.

When user has a partial compatibility pair (e.g. hypertrophy + running), the **secondary goal takes max 25–30% of total weekly training time/volume**.

## 6. SPECIFIC INCOMPATIBLE COMBINATIONS — READY MESSAGES

### Corsa + Massa + Ricomposizione (the example from your brief)
> "Questi tre obiettivi insieme non sono perseguibili in modo efficace. Correre richiede volume cardiovascolare che compete con il recupero necessario per l'ipertrofia. La ricomposizione è già di per sé un compromesso tra due obiettivi opposti. Inseguire tutti e tre contemporaneamente produrrà risultati minimi su tutti i fronti e rischia di portarti a overtraining in 4–6 settimane.
>
> Le tue opzioni realistiche:
> **Opzione 1 — Ricomposizione + Corsa leggera (2×/week):** perdi grasso e costruisci una base muscolare. La corsa supporta il deficit calorico senza interferire troppo. Risultati visibili in 10–16 settimane.
> **Opzione 2 — Massa (4–6 mesi) poi Definizione (3–4 mesi):** il percorso più efficiente a lungo termine per avere sia massa che un fisico definito. La corsa entra nella fase di definizione.
> **Opzione 3 — Solo Corsa + Mantenimento muscolare:** se la corsa è la priorità, costruisci resistenza e mantieni la massa con 2 sessioni di forza/settimana."

### Skill calisthenics + Taglio aggressivo
> "Le skill tecniche come planche e front lever richiedono freschezza neuromuscolare e adattamenti di forza che non avvengono in deficit calorico aggressivo. Un taglio aggressivo while learning skill produrrà stagnazione su entrambi i fronti.
>
> **Opzione 1:** Mantieni le calorie a mantenimento o leggero surplus (+100–200 kcal) e progredisci sulle skill. Il grasso cala lentamente come effetto collaterale dell'aumento di massa magra.
> **Opzione 2:** Finisci il taglio prima (8–12 settimane), poi attacca le skill con il corpo in condizioni ottimali."

### Principiante con 2+ obiettivi di performance
> "Con la tua esperienza attuale, inseguire [OBIETTIVO A] e [OBIETTIVO B] contemporaneamente non è efficace. Il tuo sistema nervoso e muscolare è in fase di adattamento — aggiungere troppi stimoli diversi rallenta l'apprendimento motorio e l'adattamento fisico su entrambi i fronti.
>
> Il percorso più efficiente: un obiettivo principale per i primi 3–4 mesi, poi aggiungiamo il secondo quando hai una base solida. Quale delle due cose ti sta più a cuore adesso?"
